1) The Problem We Are Solving

As seva operations scale, the current execution model remains fragmented across manual channels and repeated human coordination. This limits consistency, delays decision cycles, and weakens outcome visibility at leadership level.

Current Operational Friction

  • Volunteer intake and allocation are inconsistent across teams and sevas.
  • Communication is spread across disconnected channels, causing context loss.
  • Onboarding effort is repeatedly duplicated by coordinators and leaders.
  • Progress and impact evidence are not captured in a standard structure.

Strategic Cost of Inaction

  • Leadership bandwidth is diverted from strategy to operational firefighting.
  • Scaling without process standards increases governance and compliance risk.
  • Low-quality data reduces reliability of dashboards and planning decisions.
  • Impact storytelling becomes slower and less defensible for stakeholders.

2) The Proposed Solution

AI Sevak Portal establishes one internal operating system for the end-to-end volunteer lifecycle: registration, allocation, onboarding, collaboration, tracking, approvals, and governed impact communication.

Operational Modules

  • Volunteer profile setup, availability, and seva discovery
  • Role-based onboarding: general and seva-specific tracks
  • Calendarized execution, attendance-hours, and group messaging
  • Logs moderation, timeline publishing, and expense governance

AI Assistance (Human-Led)

  • Recommendation support for volunteer-seva fit quality
  • Onboarding Q&A from approved institutional content
  • Progress summaries for joiners, coordinators, and leadership
  • Draft narrative generation for review-led impact storytelling

Governance by Design

  • Role-based access controls (C1/C2/C3/C4/Admin)
  • Human review before official publication of updates and stories
  • Audit trails for approvals, edits, and moderation actions
  • Policy-aligned guardrails for privacy, dignity, and consent

3) Who Inside SRLC Will Use It and Benefit

The platform is designed as a role-aware internal operating layer so each function sees only what it needs while leadership retains cross-seva visibility.

Primary User Groups

  • C4 Sevaks: clear joining path, responsibilities, and status visibility.
  • C3 Coordinators: better day-to-day execution tracking and follow-up.
  • C2 Seva Leaders: reduced administrative load and stronger controls.
  • C1 Trustees: portfolio-level visibility and decision-ready summaries.
  • Platform Admins: policy enforcement, moderation, and compliance support.

Benefit by Value Type

  • Efficiency: less manual rework in onboarding and coordination.
  • Quality: standardized workflows across teams and sevas.
  • Decision Support: dependable data for planning and reviews.
  • Institutional Memory: searchable timeline of execution and learnings.
  • Transparency: trusted impact narratives from moderated evidence.

5) Key Technical and Operational Dependencies

Technical Dependencies

  • Secure authentication and role-based authorization controls.
  • Reliable data layer for profiles, logs, media metadata, and expenses.
  • Notification and reminder capability for execution workflows.
  • AI integration with transparent recommendation rationale.
  • Audit logging for approvals, moderation, and policy actions.

Operational Dependencies

  • Finalized SOPs for general and seva-specific onboarding.
  • Clear role ownership across C1/C2/C3/C4 and Admin teams.
  • Pilot participation and feedback cadence from seva leaders.
  • Data discipline for profile completeness, logs, and approvals.
  • Story governance standards for privacy, dignity, and consent.

Key Risks

  • Adoption drift back to legacy communication channels.
  • Incomplete data capture reducing AI output quality.
  • Perceived opacity in recommendation rationale.

Mitigation Strategy

  • Make portal workflows easier than legacy pathways.
  • Enforce guided forms and minimum required data fields.
  • Publish recommendation factors and preserve human override.

6) Introduction and Context

Organization Context

SRMD and SRLC run multiple seva-driven impact programs where volunteers are central to delivery. As activity scales across chapters and projects, manual registration, informal communication, repeated onboarding, and fragmented status tracking create operational friction.

Terminology

  • Seva: service initiative or project.
  • Sevak: volunteer contributing time and skills.
  • Mumukshu: mission-aligned spiritual aspirant, potentially a volunteer.
  • Seva Leader: operational owner of one or more sevas.
  • POC: point of contact for execution coordination.

7) Objectives, Scope, and Principles

Primary Objectives

  • Reduce time to onboard and allocate volunteers.
  • Improve match quality between profile and seva needs.
  • Centralize updates, collaboration, and evidence capture.
  • Enable role-based control and reliable reporting.
  • Build trusted data for leadership and donor narratives.

In Scope

  • Profile and availability capture.
  • Seva discovery and registration flow.
  • AI-assisted allocation and onboarding support.
  • Calendar, hours, messaging, logs, and expense tracking.
  • Role-based dashboards and approvals.

Out of Scope (Current Version)

  • Advanced individual performance scoring.
  • Fully automated approvals without human override.
  • Public external donor portal for all audiences.
  • Deep ERP and finance-system integration.

Product Principles

  • Human-led, AI-assisted decisioning.
  • Process discipline with compassion and flexibility.
  • Privacy-first handling of volunteer data.
  • Auditability for critical actions and updates.

9) End-to-End Product Modules (Detailed)

4.1 Registration and Profile

  • Capture personal basics, location, availability, skills, and interests.
  • Optional prior seva experience and readiness markers.
  • Output: searchable volunteer identity for allocation quality.

4.2 Seva Allocation and Discovery

  • Catalog by location, skill demand, and status.
  • Open join versus approval-based join workflows.
  • Capacity controls and over-commitment warnings.

4.3 Onboarding

  • General onboarding: mission, values, decorum, app guidance.
  • Seva-specific onboarding: SOPs, goals, contacts, live context brief.
  • AI Q&A and summary support from approved content sources.

4.4 Calendar and Time Logging

  • Daily and weekly hour entry per seva.
  • Availability checks and schedule discipline.
  • Optional external calendar sync in future iterations.

4.5 Messaging

  • Seva-scoped communication threads.
  • Role-tagged announcements and reminders.
  • Operational intent, not social-chat sprawl.

4.6 Logs and Timeline

  • Text, image, and video progress logging.
  • Moderation and publish controls by leaders/admin.
  • Review-ready daily/weekly summary generation.

4.7 Impact Storytelling and Visibility

  • Convert moderated logs to structured impact events.
  • Map activities to goals, outcomes, and contribution context.
  • Generate story cards and monthly evidence-backed digests.
  • Apply dignity, privacy, consent, and no auto-publish guardrails.

4.8 Expense Logging

  • Capture date, amount, category, purpose, and attachments.
  • Maintain role-gated status lifecycle and reviewer metadata.

10) Workflow Blueprint (Authoritative)

  1. Sevak signs up and completes profile.
  2. Sevak explores seva catalog and receives AI suggestions.
  3. Sevak joins or requests approval-based seva.
  4. Seva leader/coordinator approves where required.
  5. AI plus static kits complete onboarding readiness.
  6. Sevak logs hours, collaborates, and posts updates.
  7. Leaders/admin moderate, summarize, and monitor health.
  8. Timeline and metrics drive reporting and improvements.
  9. Approved impact stories support leadership and communication goals.

  • Onboarding knowledge base content.
  • Timeline logs and media metadata.
  • Messaging, meetings, and execution context.

Governance Controls

  • Role-based permissions across C1/C2/C3/C4/Admin.
  • Approval workflows for restricted sevas and expenses.
  • Moderation pipelines for publishable timeline content.
  • Audit logs for assignments, approvals, and critical edits.
  • Defined retention and privacy policies before production.

12) AI Boundaries, Quality, and Safety

AI Should Assist With

  • Recommendation ranking for volunteer allocation.
  • Onboarding Q&A on approved documentation.
  • Mid-cycle joiner context summaries.
  • Daily/weekly summary drafts for reviewers.

AI Should Not Decide Alone

  • Final approvals in sensitive/restricted workflows.
  • Financial approvals or policy exceptions.
  • Any governance-critical action without human review.

Safety and Quality Controls

  • Human review before broad publication.
  • Content safety checks for uploaded media.
  • Reason snippets for recommendation transparency.
