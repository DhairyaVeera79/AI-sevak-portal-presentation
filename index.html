AI Sevak Portal

A unified, AI-assisted operating platform to standardize volunteer coordination, strengthen governance, and improve measurable seva outcomes across SRLC.

Presentation Goal: Submit a complete, stakeholder-facing plan for execution by Friday, 10 April.

Delivery Horizon: Build completion by 31 August 2026, offering-ready by September 2026.

1) The Problem We Are Solving

As seva initiatives scale, current operations rely on fragmented manual processes, which limits coordination quality, accountability, and timely reporting.

Current Operational Gaps

  • Volunteer intake and allocation are inconsistent across sevas.
  • Coordination happens on disconnected channels, leading to missed updates.
  • Onboarding is repeatedly re-explained by leaders and coordinators.
  • Logs and outcomes are not uniformly structured for analysis.

Why This Matters Now

  • Leadership bandwidth is increasingly consumed by coordination overhead.
  • Scaling without standards introduces process and governance risk.
  • Data quality limits evidence-based decisions and storytelling.
  • A shared operations backbone is now mission-critical.

2) The Proposed Solution

AI Sevak Portal is a single internal system for the full volunteer lifecycle: registration, allocation, onboarding, execution, reporting, and governed storytelling.

Operational Modules

  • Volunteer profile setup and seva discovery
  • Role-based onboarding (general and seva-specific)
  • Calendar, attendance-hours logging, and seva messaging
  • Moderated logs and transparent expense management

AI Assistance (Human-Led)

  • Recommendation support for volunteer-seva fit
  • Onboarding Q&A from approved institutional content
  • Progress summaries for joiners, coordinators, and leaders

Governance by Design

  • Role-based access controls (C1/C2/C3/C4/Admin)
  • Human review before official publications and stories
  • Audit trails for approvals, edits, and moderation actions

3) Who Inside SRLC Will Use It and Benefit

Primary User Groups

  • C4 Sevaks: clear joining path, responsibilities, and status visibility.
  • C3 Coordinators: better day-to-day execution tracking and follow-up.
  • C2 Seva Leaders: reduced administrative load and stronger controls.
  • C1 Trustees: portfolio-level visibility and decision-ready summaries.
  • Platform Admins: policy enforcement, moderation, and compliance support.

Benefit by Value Type

  • Efficiency: less manual rework in onboarding and coordination.
  • Quality: standardized workflows across teams and sevas.
  • Decision Support: dependable data for planning and reviews.
  • Institutional Memory: searchable timeline of execution and learnings.
  • Transparency: trusted impact narratives from moderated evidence.

Success Criteria by September 2026

  • Majority of pilot sevas executing through standardized portal workflows.
  • Measurable reduction in repetitive onboarding and manual follow-ups.
  • Consistent weekly logging and stronger leadership visibility.
  • Trusted, moderated impact storytelling linked to operational evidence.

5) Key Technical and Operational Dependencies

Technical Dependencies

  • Secure authentication and role-based authorization controls.
  • Reliable data layer for profiles, logs, media metadata, and expenses.
  • Notification and reminder capability for execution workflows.
  • AI integration with transparent recommendation rationale.
  • Audit logging for approvals, moderation, and policy actions.

Operational Dependencies

  • Finalized SOPs for general and seva-specific onboarding.
  • Clear role ownership across C1/C2/C3/C4 and Admin teams.
  • Pilot participation and feedback cadence from seva leaders.
  • Data discipline for profile completeness, logs, and approvals.
  • Story governance standards for privacy, dignity, and consent.
