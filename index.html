01

The Problem We Are Solving

SRMD and SRLC are scaling seva across multiple initiatives, but core volunteer operations remain fragmented across manual workflows, informal communication channels, and repeated human coordination.

Current Operational Pain

  • Volunteer intake and assignment are inconsistent and difficult to audit.
  • Updates are scattered across channels, causing missed context and delays.
  • Leaders and coordinators repeatedly re-run onboarding manually.
  • Logs and outcomes are not structured enough for dependable reporting.
  • Process quality degrades as volunteer volume grows.

Why This Is Critical Now

  • Leadership capacity is consumed by avoidable coordination overhead.
  • Governance risk increases when workflows are inconsistent.
  • Data quality gaps reduce confidence in decision-making.
  • Impact communication becomes slower, weaker, and less evidence-backed.

Core Problem Statement

The current volunteer operations model is not scalable. SRLC needs a unified system that standardizes registration, allocation, onboarding, collaboration, and evidence capture while preserving human oversight.

Terminology and Context

  • Seva: a service initiative or project.
  • Sevak: volunteer contributor.
  • Mumukshu: mission-aligned aspirant, potentially a volunteer.
  • Seva Leader / POC: operational ownership and coordination roles.
02

The Proposed Solution

AI Sevak Portal is the unified internal operating system for the end-to-end volunteer lifecycle, built with a human-led, AI-assisted approach and governed decision controls.

Strategic Objectives

  • Reduce onboarding and allocation turnaround time.
  • Improve volunteer-seva match quality.
  • Centralize updates, evidence, and reporting.
  • Enable role-based operational control.
  • Create trusted data for leadership decisions.

In Scope

  • Profile and availability capture
  • Seva discovery and registration
  • AI allocation guidance and onboarding support
  • Calendar, messaging, logs, and expenses
  • Role-based dashboards and approvals

Out of Scope (Current Version)

  • Advanced individual performance scoring
  • Fully automated approvals
  • Public donor portal for all audiences
  • Deep ERP and finance integration

4.1 Registration & Profile

Capture identity, skills, availability, interests, location, and readiness markers for searchable volunteer profiles.

4.2 Seva Allocation & Discovery

Catalog-based matching with open-join and approval-based flows, capacity controls, and over-commitment checks.

4.3 Structured Onboarding

General plus seva-specific onboarding with AI Q&A and mid-cycle context summaries from approved content.

4.4 Calendar & Time Logging

Daily/weekly effort logging per seva with schedule discipline and future optional external calendar sync.

4.5 Messaging

Seva-scoped operational communication with role-tagged announcements and reminders.

4.6 Logs & Timeline

Text/image/video updates with moderation, review, and publish controls for reliable institutional memory.

4.7 Impact Storytelling

Transform moderated logs into evidence-backed impact stories with dignity, consent, and traceability guardrails.

4.8 Expense Logging

Role-gated expense submissions with attachments, reviewer metadata, and status lifecycle controls.

Workflow Blueprint (Authoritative 9-Step Flow)

  1. Sevak signs up and completes profile.
  2. Sevak explores catalog and receives AI suggestions.
  3. Sevak joins or requests approval-based seva.
  4. Leader/coordinator approves where required.
  5. Onboarding kits and AI support establish readiness.
  6. Sevak logs hours, collaborates, and posts updates.
  7. Leaders/admin moderate, summarize, and monitor health.
  8. Timeline and metrics feed reporting and improvement.
  9. Approved impact stories support leadership communication.

03

Who Inside SRLC Will Use It and Benefit

The platform is role-aware by design: each stakeholder gets relevant operational context while leadership receives cross-seva visibility for governance and strategic decisions.

User Personas

  • C1 Trustees / Senior Leadership: strategic oversight and portfolio analytics.
  • C2 Seva Leaders: planning, approvals, team health, and governance decisions.
  • C3 Coordinators / Managers: daily execution tracking and follow-up workflows.
  • C4 Sevaks: joining, onboarding, participation logs, and collaboration.
  • Platform Admin: compliance, moderation, policy controls, and system governance.

Role Capabilities

  • C4: profile, join/apply, onboarding, hour logging, updates, expense submissions.
  • C3: delegated approvals, monitoring, reminders, and operational coordination.
  • C2: define seva requirements, assign POCs, approve restricted workflows.
  • C1: cross-seva trends, escalations, and decision-ready summaries.
  • Admin: roles, moderation governance, archive/compliance policy execution.

Operational Efficiency

C2/C3 teams spend less time on repetitive coordination and manual follow-up loops.

Volunteer Experience

C4 users receive a clear, structured journey from onboarding through active seva execution.

Decision Quality

C1/C2 leaders gain reliable data for review, planning, and prioritization across sevas.

Institutional Memory

Timeline archives preserve context and reduce re-onboarding cost for new contributors.

Transparency

Moderated and traceable impact stories support trusted stakeholder communication.

Governance Integrity

Controlled permissions and audit trails improve accountability across critical workflows.

05

Key Technical and Operational Dependencies

This section consolidates dependencies, governance boundaries, risks, open decisions, and immediate next actions required for successful delivery.

Technical Dependencies

  • Secure authentication and role-based authorization model.
  • Reliable storage for profile data, logs, media, and expenses.
  • Notification mechanisms for reminders and approvals.
  • AI integration for recommendations and summarization.
  • Audit logging and moderation controls for governance.

Operational Dependencies

  • Finalized SOP content for onboarding and execution.
  • Clear ownership across C1/C2/C3/C4 and Admin roles.
  • Seva leader participation in pilot and feedback loops.
  • Data discipline for profile, logs, and approval workflows.
  • Story governance for review, consent, privacy, and publishing.

Data and Governance Controls

  • Core data domains: profiles, catalog, logs, messaging, expenses.
  • Approval workflows for restricted sevas and reimbursements.
  • Moderation queue before publishable timeline outputs.
  • Retention and privacy policy definition before production release.

AI Boundaries and Safety

  • AI assists with ranking, Q&A, and summaries.
  • AI does not make final approval or financial decisions.
  • Human review required before broad publication.
  • Recommendation rationale must be transparent.

Risk and Mitigation

  • Adoption risk: keep portal easier than legacy channels.
  • Data quality risk: guided forms and required fields.
  • AI trust risk: show factors and preserve override rights.
  • Governance risk: enforce moderation queues and audit trails.

Open Decisions Before Production Build

  • Final technical stack choices for web/mobile/backend boundaries.
  • Notification channel strategy and frequency policy.
  • Module-level data retention windows.
  • Cross-team integration ownership and accountability boundaries.
  • Scope of external visibility and approval gates.

Immediate Next Steps (By Team)

  • Product & Program: finalize user stories, acceptance criteria, SOP package.
  • Engineering & Data: lock architecture and release quality gates.
  • Operations & Governance: finalize moderation, privacy, and policy controls.
  • Cross-Team: publish milestone cadence and escalate blockers weekly.
  • Documentation: role matrix, data dictionary, and screen inventory completion.
